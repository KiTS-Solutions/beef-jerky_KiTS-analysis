PART TWO
Delivery Principles — How to Present
THE FIVE FUNDAMENTALS
How You Deliver Is As Important As What You Say
01
PACE — Always Slower
Nervousness accelerates speech. Consciously slow down. Pause after every key statistic. Two seconds of silence after "ZERO local competitors" is worth more than any sentence that follows it.
02
POSTURE — Open & Still
Upright. Hands visible. No crossed arms. Do not fidget with the laptop. Do not sway. Stillness reads as authority. Your body language is communicating before your voice does.
03
EYE CONTACT — To Them
80% of your eye contact goes to the client — not the screen. Glance at the slide to cue the transition, then return to them. The slide is support. You are the presentation.
04
SILENCE — Weaponize It
After a powerful number or claim — stop talking. Let it sit. The discomfort you feel in that silence is exactly what makes it work. The next person who speaks owns the moment. Make it them.
05
LISTEN — To Their Reactions
Read them in real time. A nod means they agree — build on it. A frown means confusion or concern — address it immediately. The best presenters are also the best observers in the room.
06
VOICE — Lower and Project
Lower pitch signals authority. High pitch signals nervousness. Project from the chest, not the throat. Vary pace — speed up slightly on supporting detail, slow down on key claims and numbers.
ABSOLUTE RULES
What You Must Never Do
  • ✗Never say "Um," "basically," "honestly," or "like" — pause instead. One conscious pause is worth ten filler words.
  • ✗Never apologize for the material. Not once. Not for the length, not for a number, not for a correction. Own everything.
  • ✗Never look at the screen for more than 3 consecutive seconds. Eyes back to the client.
  • ✗Never answer a question before they have finished asking it. Wait. Breathe. Then respond.
  • ✗Never fill silence after asking for the decision. You have asked. Now wait. The next word spoken is theirs.
  • ✗Never check your phone — not once, not even while they are reading the slide.
  • ✗Never volunteer bad news you haven't been asked about. The nutritional panel blocker is known — acknowledge it if raised, do not raise it first.
  • ✗Never over-explain a complete answer. Say the answer. Stop. Silence signals confidence, not incompleteness.
IF INTERRUPTED OR CHALLENGED
Recovery & Composure Protocols
WHEN INTERRUPTED MID-SLIDE

"Good question — let me address that now. We have a slide coming that covers exactly this — would you like to go there directly, or shall I answer briefly and we continue the flow?"

WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW THE ANSWER

WHEN THEY SEEM DISENGAGED

"Before I continue — does this resonate with the direction you had in mind? I want to make sure we're covering what matters most to you."

HOW TO HANDLE Q&A — THE RULES

Do not read the answers from the slide. Know them. Internalize them. Deliver with confidence and brevity — then stop talking. Silence after a complete answer is a sign of strength, not uncertainty. Wait until they finish asking before you begin answering.

Q1 — "Why should I give full control to an advisory firm I just met?"
You are not giving control — you are giving authority to execute within parameters you define. Every major financial commitment and every regulatory filing requires your approval. What you are giving is the mandate to move without waiting for a meeting every time we need to visit an account. The 10 deliverables in front of you today were built before you agreed to anything. That is what full commitment looks like before a contract exists.
EMPHASIS: The 10 deliverables are the proof. Reference them again here.
Q2 — "What happens if the product doesn't sell?"
Every first account is sale-or-return — no retailer takes financial risk on your product. We do not launch until MoPH approval is confirmed, the brand is professionally executed, and the product is lab-verified. Revenue projections are conservative, assuming 20–40 units per week per gym account at steady state. If sell-through is below target, we have a formal feedback and pricing review protocol built into the mandate — not improvised after the fact.
KEY WORD: sale-or-return. Repeat it if needed.
Q3 — "How long before I start making money?" ★ ALWAYS ASKED
Under the recommended Tier 2 scenario with corrected projections: breakeven is targeted between Month 8 and Month 11. By Month 6, revenue is projected at $9,000 to $14,000 per month at a gross margin of 55 to 62%. By Month 12, revenue is projected at $40,000 to $65,000 per month. These are directional projections based on real Lebanese channel data and competitive pricing research — not guarantees.
★ SAY THESE WITHOUT LOOKING AT THE SLIDE: $42K in · M8–11 breakeven · 55–62% GM · $9K–14K M6 · $40K–65K M12
Q4 — "What if I want to manage some parts of this myself?"
That is your decision and we will document the division clearly in the mandate. However, our methodology is an integrated system — the outreach script connects to the commercial proposal, which connects to the account tracker, which drives the weekly check-in protocol. The more elements are separated, the more the system loses its compounding effect. Our recommendation: run the full system for 6 months, evaluate results, then decide what to internalise based on demonstrated performance.
FRAME: Not a refusal. A professional recommendation with a trial period built in.
Q5 — "What is the minimum I can invest to get started?"
Tier 1 at $18,500 is the viable minimum — it registers the entity, begins MoPH registration for one SKU, and gives you market presence. However, RU2YA's recommendation is Tier 2 at $42,000. At Tier 1, there is a real risk that the packaging appears underfunded relative to Jack Link's and Wild West on the same shelf. The difference between Tier 1 and Tier 2 is the difference between entering the market and owning the category.
MEMORABLE LINE: "Tier 1 enters the market. Tier 2 owns the category." Say it slowly.
Q6 — "Why Lebanon and not start with another market?"
Lebanon is the proof-of-concept that unlocks the GCC. "Made in Lebanon" carries premium weight in UAE and Saudi Arabia — the provenance story is a marketing asset. Halal certification from Dar Al-Fatwa Lebanon is recognized across the GCC. Every product decision — the flavor, the certification, the trilingual packaging — was designed for Lebanon first and GCC second. Starting in Lebanon is not a constraint. It is the strategy.
FINAL WORD: "It is the strategy." Pause after it.
Q7 — "What does RU2YA do that I couldn't hire someone else to do?"
An employee executes tasks. RU2YA brings the full system: the research base, the brand work already done, the outreach scripts, the financial model, the manufacturer brief, the regulatory knowledge, and the personal relationship network across Lebanese gyms and nutrition stores. Rebuilding this from scratch would take 6–9 months and cost more than Tier 3. You would also be paying for someone to learn the market while we already know it cold.
FRAME: Not a comparison — a replacement cost argument. What would building this yourself actually cost?

AFTER THE CLOSING LINE — THE MOST IMPORTANT INSTRUCTION

Stop talking. Entirely. Do not add a qualifier. Do not soften. Do not ask "So, what do you think?" Let the room hold the closing line for 5–10 full seconds.

Then ask the tier question directly: "Which scenario are we working with?"

Then silence again. The next person who speaks is not you.

WHAT NOT TO DO

Do not renegotiate before they have responded. Do not offer discounts or scope adjustments unprompted. Do not re-explain the deck. Do not say "I know this is a big decision." If they need a moment to think — let them think in silence. You have made your case.

IF THEY DO NOT SIGN TODAY

This is acceptable and manageable. Offer to leave all 10 deliverables with them. Say: "Everything we walked through today — all ten documents — I am leaving with you. Read them tonight. Every question you might have after this meeting has already been answered somewhere in that body of work."

Then agree on a specific follow-up date and time before leaving the room. A specific time — not "sometime next week."

IF THEY SAY YES TODAY

Do not celebrate visibly. Nod. Say: "Good. Here is how we begin." Then outline the immediate next three actions: (1) Mandate signed and dated, (2) Manufacturer contacted within 5 business days for nutritional panel, (3) SARL registration initiated by next week. Move from pitch mode to execution mode immediately.
