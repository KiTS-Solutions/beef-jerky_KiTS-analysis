# STRIKE BITES — Lebanon Launch Financial Research Study
### Prepared as investment-memo input for Ru'ya 360 / KITS Advisory Group

## TL;DR
- A realistic 6–12 month launch budget for STRIKE BITES in Lebanon lands the project between the **Standard ($42,000)** and **Full ($82,000)** tiers; the **Lean $18,500** tier is viable only as a "soft pilot" covering legal setup, certifications, one salesperson and minimal collateral — it cannot fund the 60–80 account rollout the brief envisions.
- The biggest swing factors are **people cost** (an FMCG field sales team plus Ru'ya 360's own engagement overhead) and **sale-or-return cash-flow drag** — not certifications or hard costs; halal/SFDA work and GCC listing fees are Phase-4 items that must be ring-fenced from the launch budget.
- Lebanon's macro backdrop is unusually stable for budgeting: the LBP has been fixed at **89,500/USD on the BDL balance sheet since 31 January 2024**, the economy is dollarized, VAT is 11% and corporate tax 17% — but cash-only/short-credit retail terms and Circular-158/FATF-grey-list banking friction make **working capital, not the P&L, the real risk**.

## Key Findings
1. **Currency is stable and the economy is dollarized.** Per BDL Central Council Decision No. 48/4/24 (15 Feb 2024), "the official exchange rate of 89,500 LBP per dollar has been adopted in the BdL Balance sheet starting January 31st 2024." (The earlier Feb 2023 reset moved the rate to 15,000 LBP/USD before the later move to 89,500.) The rate held in a narrow 89,503–89,763 band over the trailing 52 weeks. **Budget entirely in USD; FX risk is low.**
2. **Labor is cheap in USD but loaded with employer charges.** Minimum wage rose to **LBP 28,000,000/month (~$313)** plus a daily-workers' minimum of LBP 1,300,000, effective 1 August 2025 under Ministry of Labor Decree #699 (Official Gazette #32, 24 July 2025). Average gross salary in early 2026 is ~$270–300/month.
3. **VAT 11%, corporate income tax 17%, SARL minimum capital LBP 5,000,000 (~$3,333).** Per PwC, mandatory VAT registration now only applies above LBP 5 billion turnover (raised from LBP 100 million by the 2024 Budget Law), so a startup can defer VAT registration.
4. **Lebanese lab testing is inexpensive:** ~**$25 per meat micro panel** and ~**$15 per nutritional label panel** at accredited chamber labs; halal (GSO 2055-2) and SFDA/UAE registration are the expensive, GCC-phase items.
5. **GCC entry is costly and slow** — UAE hypermarket listing fees run **AED 5,000–15,000 (~$1,400–4,100) per SKU** at Carrefour/Lulu (Bagason Group), with distributor margins of 15–25% stacking on top of 25–50% retailer margins.

## Details

### SECTION 3 — Lebanon Macro Realities (anchors the whole model)
- **Exchange rate:** 89,500 LBP/USD adopted on the BDL balance sheet from 31 Jan 2024 (BDL Decision 48/4/24), holding ~89,600–89,700 through 2024–2025; 52-week range 89,503–89,763. Plan in USD.
- **Inflation:** Hyperinflation has eased sharply from crisis peaks into 2025–26; budget moderate single-digit USD-cost creep on local services (verify at contracting).
- **Minimum wage:** LBP 28,000,000/month (~$313) since 1 Aug 2025 (Decree #699); daily-worker minimum LBP 1,300,000.
- **Working hours:** 48 hrs/week (Labour Code Art. 10), 8 hrs/day over 6 days; overtime 150%, holidays 200%; daily cap 12 hrs; 15 days paid annual leave after 1 year of service.
- **NSSF / employer charges:** Per PwC Worldwide Tax Summaries — **8%** maternity/sickness (max base LBP 120m → max LBP 9.6m/mo), **6%** family allowance (max LBP 1.08m/mo), **8.5%** end-of-service indemnity (no ceiling); employee pays 3%. Effective employer load ≈ 22.5% (plus the uncapped EOSI accrual).
- **VAT:** 11% standard; exports zero-rated; mandatory registration only above LBP 5bn turnover (2024 Budget Law) — a launch-stage SARL can stay unregistered initially.
- **Corporate income tax:** 17% on real profits (mandatory real-profit method for SARL/SAL); dividend WHT 10%.
- **Payment terms:** Lebanese retail is largely cash or very short credit post-crisis (banks require ~100% cash collateral for importer LCs). Assume NET 0–30 in modern trade, cash in traditional trade.
- **Sale-or-return drag:** With consignment into gyms and nutrition stores, first cash typically returns 30–60+ days after placement; model **~2–3 months** before initial restock revenue recycles.

### SECTION 1A — Transportation & Logistics
- **Fuel:** 95-octane ~$1.21–1.32/liter (≈LBP 119,250/liter, Apr 2026). A route covering 60–80 accounts across Beirut, Metn, Keserwan, Jounieh and Tripoli consumes roughly 250–400 liters/month → **~$350–550/month fuel**.
- **Vehicle:** Used small van rental or amortized purchase, all-in **~$400–700/month**; commercial auto insurance is low-cost in USD terms.
- **Cold chain:** Beef jerky is **shelf-stable (ambient)** if water activity ≤0.85 with barrier packaging — **no refrigerated chain needed**, a major saving; control warehouse temperature/humidity and avoid summer heat abuse to protect shelf life.
- **Packaging imports from Turkey:** The Lebanon–Turkey FTA was signed in 2010 but remains **unratified**, so Turkish pouches/films/cartons enter under standard MFN tariffs + 11% VAT on CIF value + customs fees; budget realistic landed duty/VAT.
- **Courier/local delivery:** Per-drop courier is viable but an in-house route is more economical at 60–80 accounts.

### SECTION 1B — Non-Digital Marketing
In-gym branded display stands/mini-fridges, trade presentation binders, sampling at gyms/CrossFit/expos, event sponsorships, branded apparel (shirts/caps), gym-trainer incentive and referral programs, POS materials (shelf talkers, price cards, posters), business cards, trade-show participation, and print/gym-newsletter ads. These are highly negotiable and scalable; a credible non-digital activation budget for a 6-month launch ranges from a few thousand dollars (Lean) to low-five-figures (Full).

### SECTION 1C — Operational Overhead
- **Workspace:** Beirut coworking dedicated desk **$209–329/month** (Regus); private offices from **$255/person/month** (Regus); independent coworking memberships **$150–400/month**, private offices **$300–800/month**. Start at **~$300–600/month** for a small team.
- **Utilities/comms/generator/storage:** Lebanon's grid is unreliable; private-generator subscription ("ishtirak") + fuel is a real monthly line. Budget combined utilities + SIM/data/WhatsApp Business + internet + stationery **~$300–600/month** for a small team/warehouse.

### SECTION 1D — Insurance
Product-liability (food), commercial-vehicle, and property/public-liability cover are all available locally at modest USD cost; **food product liability is the priority cover** before retail placement.

### SECTION 1E — Banking & Financial Operations
Hold operating cash in **fresh-dollar** accounts and, ideally, an **offshore USD account (Cyprus/UAE)** for clean international supplier payments. Circular 158 (depositor USD disbursement) and the **FATF grey-listing (June 2023, per US ITA, for AML/CFT deficiencies)** add cross-border transfer friction — factor wire fees and compliance delays into procurement timing.

### SECTION 1F — Quality Assurance & Food Safety (Lebanese hard figures)
- **Microbiological meat panel: ~$25/sample** at accredited Lebanese chamber labs (CCIAS Food Safety Laboratories, Saida — ISO 17025, 2025 price list); single organism or single chemical test **$7 each**; **nutritional facts label panel $15**. Full food-safety + label package per SKU at chamber rates ≈ **$40–70**; the national reference lab **IRI is likely 1.5–3× higher** (estimate).
- **IRI** publishes no per-test tariff but offers **$100/year industrial membership** for testing discounts.
- **Shelf-life/stability:** no published Lebanese price; international benchmark (MSU Extension) **$400–800** for a basic shelf-stable study, **$900–2,000+** if a pathogen challenge study is required. Budget several hundred USD per SKU.
- **LIBNOR NL Conformity Mark:** voluntary for jerky; application/audit/analysis fees exist but are not published.
- **SGS halal (GSO 2055-2):** priced per audit-day; realistic initial certification **~$2,000–5,000** for a small meat manufacturer plus **~$1,000–2,500/yr** surveillance (30–60% of initial). GCC-portable, GAC-accredited, SFDA-recognized.
- **SASO/SFDA (Saudi):** SFDA food consignment certification (Intertek) **$550–3,500 per consignment** by value band + $100–125/container sealing; product/establishment registration via FIRS; importer must be Saudi-based.
- **MoPH/Ministry registration:** for a plain meat snack the relevant track is **Ministry of Agriculture animal-products health registration** (inspection-based) plus municipal/MoPH facility health attestation; fees are modest but not publicly listed — obtain by direct quote.

### SECTION 1G — Technology & Operations Tools
Accounting software (QuickBooks/Zoho Books/local), simple inventory management, a mobile order-management/POS for reps, a lightweight CRM, an informational (non-ecommerce) website (hosting + basic design), and print-ready brand/logo collateral. All are low-cost SaaS/one-off items; budget a modest monthly SaaS stack plus a one-time website/branding spend.

### SECTION 2 — Ru'ya 360 Team Structure & Costs
Anchored on the ~$313 minimum wage, ~$270–300 average gross salary (early 2026) and ~22.5% employer charges:
- **2A Sales & Account Management:** To open and maintain 60–80 accounts over 6 months, plan **2 field reps** (each realistically services ~30–40 accounts) plus a **part-time sales manager/lead**. A qualified FMCG field rep earns roughly **$600–1,200/month base** plus commission (standard Lebanese FMCG commission 2–5% of collected sales). Sales coordinator (order processing/admin) **~$500–800/month**.
- **2B Design:** Brand/packaging designer freelance one-off **~$1,500–4,000** (agency multiples higher); ongoing graphic designer on retainer for POS/trade materials; a **label-compliance designer** for Arabic regulatory text + nutritional panel; product photographer **per-shoot ~$300–700**; budget 2–3 revision rounds.
- **2C QA/Food Safety:** At launch scale, **outsource** to a Lebanese lab + a part-time food-safety consultant rather than hire full-time QA; food-safety training for production staff is a modest one-off.
- **2D Regulatory & Legal:** Trademark filing (Class 29 goods + Class 35 retail) — Lebanese official fees are low but attorney fees dominate; **SARL formation all-in ≈ USD 7,500** per PwC ("includes lawyer's fees and registration fees… should not normally exceed 1% of the value of capital"), incl. the mandatory lawyer retainer; food regulatory consultant for ministry registration; ongoing legal retainer (mandatory for SARL).
- **2E Finance/Accounting:** Outsourced bookkeeper **~$200–500/month**; accountant quarterly/monthly; auditor mandatory once capital >LBP 30m or turnover threshold; financial controller only at later scale.
- **2F Operations/Logistics:** Operations coordinator; delivery driver **~$500–800/month**; warehouse manager only if volume warrants; procurement coordinator part-time/blended.
- **2G Marketing/Activation:** Field-activation coordinator; trainer-ambassador program coordinator (a roster of gym trainers receiving product + referral incentives); event coordinator for sampling; PR/media optional at launch.
- **2H Ru'ya 360 Engagement Overhead:** Project manager (partial allocation), senior advisor time (billed as % of project or day-rate retainer), admin support, travel/expenses for client/account visits, and client reporting tools/templates. Advisory/management fee for a food-startup launch typically runs as a percentage of total project value.

### SECTION 4 — Food Industry Benchmarks
- **COGS:** Beef (raw material) dominates jerky COGS — commonly **50–80%+** of COGS; as a share of revenue, quality jerky COGS typically runs **25–50%** depending on pricing power.
- **Gross margin:** Premium jerky/protein-snack gross margins commonly **35–60%**; specialty small-batch targets ~50%+.
- **Marketing spend:** Year-1 food startups run elevated marketing as a % of revenue; **trade spend alone ~9% of gross revenue** is a common CPG benchmark.
- **Distribution cost (MENA FMCG):** Distributor margins 5–25% (FMCG often 5–12%); retailer margins 25–50%.
- **Advisory/management fee:** Typically a percentage of total project value for a launch engagement.
- **Inventory turnover:** Premium snack target ~5× per year.
- **Breakeven:** With a $40–80k investment, a disciplined single-market launch can target breakeven within **12–18 months** if account rollout and restock velocity hit plan.

### SECTION 5 — GCC / MENA Expansion (Phase 4)
- **Freight Beirut→Jebel Ali:** 20-ft container ~$1,200–1,700; 40-ft ~$1,800–2,500 (jerky is light, so LCL is likely cheaper per pallet); road freight UAE→other GCC ~AED 700–1,500/pallet.
- **UAE registration:** Dubai Municipality FIRS/Montaji + ZAD product registration ~AED 500–1,500 per product; Arabic+English labeling mandatory; halal cert required for meat; mislabeling fines AED 10,000–100,000.
- **Saudi (SFDA):** Importer must be Saudi-based; SFDA establishment + product registration via FIRS; consignment certification $550–3,500 by value band.
- **GCC margins & listing fees:** Distributor 15–25% + retailer 25–50%; **UAE hypermarket listing fees AED 5,000–15,000 (~$1,400–4,100) per SKU at Carrefour/Lulu** (Bagason Group), with annual renewals AED 2,000–8,000 — a material Phase-4 capital barrier that multiplies across SKUs.

## Recommendations
1. **Select tier by ambition, not by hard costs.** Recommend the **Standard $42,000 tier** as the base case for a genuine 60–80 account Lebanon launch (2 reps + coordinator, outsourced QA/finance/design, full legal/certification, 6-month activation), stepping up to the **Full $82,000 tier** if the board wants faster account capture, deeper trade marketing, and a working-capital cushion for sale-or-return. Treat **Lean $18,500** strictly as a setup-and-pilot tranche (SARL formation ~$7,500, trademark, lab testing, one rep, minimal collateral).
2. **Ring-fence GCC costs.** Halal (GSO 2055-2 ~$2,000–5,000), SFDA, and UAE listing fees belong in a separate **Phase-4 budget**, not the launch budget — they would consume the entire Lean tier on their own.
3. **Manage working capital, not just the P&L.** Hold cash in fresh-dollar/offshore USD accounts; negotiate cash or NET-30 in modern trade; cap consignment exposure per account; model 2–3 months before restock revenue recycles.
4. **Outsource specialist functions at launch** (QA, accounting, design, regulatory) and keep only sales + ops headcount in-house; revisit in-house QA/controller hires only after multi-SKU scale.
5. **Thresholds that should trigger a plan change:** gross margin below ~45% after landed packaging duties → re-price or renegotiate the co-packer; rep productivity below ~25 active accounts/rep by month 4 → pause hiring and fix the sales motion; sale-or-return shrink above ~10% → shift to cash terms before expanding.

## Caveats
- Many Lebanese official bodies (IRI, LIBNOR, SGS Lebanon, MoPH) do not publish tariffs; several figures here come from accredited chamber-lab price lists, consultancy guides, or international benchmarks explicitly flagged as proxies — **confirm by direct quote before finalizing the memo**.
- Salary and overhead ranges reflect a volatile post-crisis labor market; verify current quotes at contracting time.
- Lebanon's macro stability depends on continued BDL policy and political conditions (parliamentary elections scheduled May 2026); a renewed FX shock would change USD-cost assumptions.
- Industry margin/marketing/jerky benchmarks are drawn largely from US/global CPG sources and should be treated as directional for the Lebanese market.
- The SARL ~$7,500 all-in figure (PwC) is higher than some low-end formation quotes (~$3,400 minimum capital plus statutory fees); the difference is principally the level of legal/advisory service — budget toward the PwC figure for a properly papered launch.
