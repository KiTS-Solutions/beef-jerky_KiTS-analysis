# STRIKE BITES — Lebanon Market Entry Financial Feasibility Validation Report

## TL;DR
- **The KITS Tier 2 ($42K) plan is broadly defensible, but the proposed $2.50–$3.50 retail price is materially UNDER-PRICED versus the validated Lebanese competitive set** (Jack Link's 40g at $5.15–$5.62, Quest Bar at $5.00, Barebells 55g at $3.89–$4.16). A $4.00–$4.75 RRP is the realistic premium-positioned shelf price and would lift gross margin by 8–15 percentage points without losing competitive position.
- **The single largest financial risk to the model is not regulatory or branding — it is the M6 revenue ramp** ($18K–$28K at 60–80 accounts implies 3–8 units/account/day, which is aggressive for a new SKU at a gym counter; realistic velocity is 1–3 units/account/day). Revenue should be re-modelled at ~50% of the current M6 figure, with the higher number reserved as an upside case.
- **Two regulatory line items have NO publicly published fee schedule and must not be presented to the board as verified numbers**: MoPH food registration per SKU and GS1 Lebanon membership/GTIN. Both require a direct written quote before the board meeting. All other study figures are within defensible ranges with the corrections noted below.

## Details

### Section 1 — Competitor Shelf Pricing (CRITICAL)

The single most important finding is that the KITS pricing assumption is wrong by ~40–60%. Verified Lebanese shelf prices:

- **Jack Link's Beef Jerky 40g — $5.62 USD** at LivGood Lebanon (Shopify metadata `og:price:amount: 5.62`, `og:price:currency: USD`, livgood.com/products/jacklinks-bf-sweet-and-hot-jerky). Carrefour Lebanon lists Teriyaki 40g at 461,446 LBP (~$5.16 at 89,500 LBP/USD), currently OUT OF STOCK on the Teriyaki SKU — indicating supply friction in the mass channel. This is the most directly comparable benchmark — a premium imported jerky in the same 40g format.
- **Quest Protein Bar (single) — $5.00 USD per bar** at Protein District Lebanon (price confirmed via og:price metadata on proteindistrictlb.com/products/quest-protein-bar). Protein District is Lebanon's #1 specialty supplement retailer.
- **Barebells Protein Bar 55g — $3.89 (Kelchi.com Caramel Cashew SKU 7340001800494) to $4.16 (LivGood Cookies & Cream)**, also stocked at Spinneys Lebanon and pharmacies (Bou Khalil, Mazen Pharmacy per brate.com).
- **Wild West Beef Jerky — NOT stocked in Lebanon.** Multiple Lebanese retailer searches (Carrefour Lebanon, LivGood, Kelchi, Spinneys Lebanon, brate.com Lebanon) returned no Wild West SKU. New World Foods' Gulfood January 2024 press release explicitly limits Wild West Halal presence to "Spinneys, Enoch, Carrefour and Lulu across the UAE" — no Lebanon mention. **The study's assumption that Wild West competes head-to-head in Lebanon is incorrect; the actual competitive set is Jack Link's + protein bars, and Lebanon is effectively green-field for a Halal-native local jerky.**

**Pricing recommendation:** Re-set Strike Bites RRP to **$4.00–$4.75 per 40g cube pack**. This positions ~10–25% below imported Jack Link's (defensible: locally produced, Halal-native, no MrBeast-branded packaging mismatch), at parity with Quest single bars, and ~20% above Barebells — appropriate for meat-protein vs. confected-protein. The $1.00–$1.75 added price falls almost entirely to gross margin, lifting GM from 38–45% (realistic at $3.00 RRP) to **55–62% at $4.50 RRP**, finally putting the study's 48–55% target within reach.

### Section 2 — Lebanese Regulatory and Legal Costs

- **SARL registration:** Per the Beirut Traders Association official guide: LBP 750,000 financial tax + 3/1000 of capital + LBP 375,000 judicial fees + 1.5/1000 + BBA 1/1000 + nominal stamps. In post-2023 USD terms with mandatory lawyer retainer (capital > LBP 50M), all-in cost realistically $1,500–$3,500. Study **VALIDATED**.
- **MoPH food registration per SKU:** Lebanese MoPH publishes a detailed pharmaceutical registration regime (Drug Registration Technical Committee, decisions 51/1, 945/1, exchange-rate-based pricing 2026/2/13696) but **no public fee schedule for packaged-food product registration exists on moph.gov.lb**, nor in USDA FSIS Lebanon library, US ITA Country Commercial Guide, or SABA IP's Lebanon food regulations brief. **This figure cannot be presented to the board as verified.** It must be obtained from MoPH Food Safety Department or a Lebanese food-import broker. Working placeholder: $300–$1,000/SKU. For comparison, per PharmaKnowl's official SFDA fee schedule (updated Aug 2025), Saudi standard food products carry no SFDA registration fee, but "Special food registration (With vitamins or special formulas): SAR 5,000/product application" (~$1,333) and food supplements SAR 5,000/item — meaning beef jerky with macro/protein claims may classify as "special" in Saudi and other GCC markets, with similar uplift potentially applicable in Lebanon.
- **Lab testing:** Industrial Research Institute (Lebanon's national lab) and Beirut private labs perform full nutritional panels for $200–$500/SKU. Study **VALIDATED**.
- **Halal certification:** Lebanon has no unified national halal authority; Dar Al-Fatwa (senior Sunni institution) provides endorsement. Single-product Dar Al-Fatwa endorsement is in the low hundreds of USD; GCC-portable certification via SGS Gulf Ltd (Sin El Fil/Horch Tabet office, SASO + ESMA + SFDA approved per sgs.com/en-lb) typically runs $1,500–$3,000 for facility audit. **Study $400–$1,200 sufficient for Lebanese-domestic only; UNDER-BUDGETED if GCC-portable required at launch.**
- **Trademark registration:** Per Country Index 2025 fee schedule citing the Lebanese IP Protection Office: filing 1 class 10,500,000 LBP + b/w publication 1,300,000 LBP + certificate 4,600,000 LBP = ~16.4M LBP ≈ $183 per class at current FX. Additional class: 10.2M LBP + 500K = ~$120. Adding $400–$800 IP attorney fee per case (Lexology / NJQ & Associates): **Class 29 only $600–$1,000; Classes 29+35 $750–$1,300**. The 2024 LIPO update flagged "extension of deadlines" due to staff shortages — registration realistically 4–8 months vs. 2–3 months pre-crisis. Note: trademarks registered or renewed on/after Feb 15, 2024 are valid for 10 years (down from 15).
- **GS1 Lebanon (gs1lb.org):** No public fee schedule; site is a thin brochure page. Direct quote required from customers@gs1lb.org / +961 1 744 161. Benchmark: GS1 Albania one-off ALL 15,000 (~$155) + annual licence by company size. Realistic placeholder $150–$300 reg + $200–$500/yr.
- **Tax ID + NSSF:** Nominal LBP fees ~$100–$300 all-in. Study VALIDATED on high side.
- **IP lawyer fees:** $400–$800 per class filing at Lebanese IP firms (NJQ & Associates, Saba IP); annual retainer optional. Study figures within market.

### Section 3 — Channel Economics

- **Sports nutrition shops** (Protein District, Sport Nutrition Lebanon, The Supplement Lab, Proteinji): 30–40% margin MENA-standard. Study 30–35% **VALIDATED**. Sale-or-return on first order feasible with independents; 30-unit MOQ realistic.
- **Gyms / CrossFit boxes:** Lebanon's CrossFit ecosystem is small per CrossFit affiliate locator. Gym nutrition counters typically take 30–35% — above the study's 25–30%. **Adjust to 30%.**
- **Pharmacies:** 30–35% consistent with Lebanese parapharma practice. Brate.com confirms Barebells distribution into pharmacy channel (Bou Khalil, Mazen Pharmacy, Dermapro).
- **Modern trade:** Per Bagason Group's published UAE/MENA distribution guide: "Retailers typically require 25–40% gross margin depending on category... Marketing and promotional support: Buyers expect promotional investment — either a promotional budget, in-store activation commitment, or listing fee (particularly for new category entrants)." Spinneys Lebanon operates 16 stores; Carrefour Lebanon, TSC, Bou Khalil also key. **35–40% margin VALIDATED but study MISSING an explicit listing-fee allowance of $500–$2,000 per chain.**
- **Lebanese FMCG distributors:** Per FDC's own corporate profile: "shaped by our team of 650 professionals... reach extends to more than 7,000 outlets all over the country" — confirming the scale of the dominant national distributor. Transmed (FMCG-only since 1946), Khalil Fattal & Fils, and FDC are the three dominant players. Distributor margins for snack FMCG in Lebanon typically run 18–25%. **Adjust study from 15–20% to 20% base case.**

### Section 4 — Production and Supply Chain

- **Lebanese beef pricing:** Spinneys Lebanon retail confirms local beef habra at $11.99–$13.50/kg, US beef short rib $39.99/kg, imported beef mince ~$5–$8/kg. Selina Wamucii reports Lebanon beef export price $4.99/kg in 2021. Industry standard 2.5–3.0 kg fresh = 1 kg dried jerky. **Per-unit beef cost for a 40g cube: $0.50–$0.95 depending on input choice.** With seasonings, packaging, labour, energy, and overhead, realistic FOB cost-per-unit is $1.10–$1.50 for premium positioning, $0.90–$1.20 for value-engineered. **Study's implied ~$1.00 is achievable but tight; model $1.20 base case.**
- **First production batch:** Tier 2's $8K–$15K for 90 days = 5,300–12,500 units depending on cost. Consistent with M6 account targets IF velocity is realistic (see Section 7).
- **Packaging:** Lebanese minimum print runs for stand-up barrier pouches 5,000–10,000 units. Study's $1,500–$2,500 (Tier 2) tight; consider Turkey or UAE sourcing (4–6 week lead time, often cheaper at scale).
- **Warehousing:** Ambient food-safe storage Greater Beirut ~$8–$15/m²/month. Study's $800–$1,500 for 6 months consistent with 10–15 m² — adequate.
- **Logistics:** Direct delivery across Greater Beirut realistic $15–$25/day for small van; budget $400–$600/month at Tier 2.

### Section 7 — Revenue Projection Stress Test (HIGHEST-RISK SECTION)

Tier 2 M6 projection: $18K–$28K at 60–80 accounts = $225–$467/account/month. At $2.16 wholesale ($3.00 RRP × 0.72): **104–216 units/account/month, or 3.5–7.2 units/account/day**.

For a brand-new SKU at M6 in a gym nutrition counter or specialty nutrition shop, realistic velocity benchmark is **0.5–2 units/account/day, with top-quartile hitting 3–5**. The KITS projection requires AVERAGE account to perform at top-quartile velocity — implausible at M6.

**Re-modelled Tier 2 M6 base case:**
- 60–80 accounts × 1.5 units/account/day × 30 days = 2,700–3,600 units/month
- At corrected $4.50 RRP × 0.70 wholesale = $3.15/unit
- M6 revenue = **$8,500–$11,300/month** (vs. original $18K–$28K)
- Retain higher figure as UPSIDE scenario contingent on >25% repeat rate

Breakeven shifts from Month 5–7 to **Month 8–11** under corrected velocity. For M12 ($40K–$65K at 120–160 accounts = $250–$542/account/month), velocity required is 2–4.5 units/account/day at corrected RRP — achievable if traction materializes, but contingent on the M6–M12 ramp curve.

### Section 8 — Lebanese Market Size

- **Gyms:** Estimated 250–400 gyms and fitness studios nationally; ~25–45 CrossFit-branded boxes per CrossFit affiliate locator, concentrated Greater Beirut.
- **Sports nutrition shops:** Realistic universe 30–60 specialty stores nationally. The 60–80 M6 account target requires gyms (majority) + nutrition shops + cafés/healthy retailers.
- **Pharmacies:** Per OPL records cited in a peer-reviewed study published in Human Resources for Health (Springer/PMC, 2019): "OPL records indicated a total number of 8855 registered pharmacists and 3198 licensed pharmacies serving an estimated population of around four million individuals." Of these ~3,198 licensed pharmacies, those carrying health/wellness food products realistically number 200–400.
- **Fitness community:** Estimated 150,000–250,000 active gym members; 3,000–5,000 dedicated CrossFit athletes; growing macro-tracking community on Lebanese fitness Instagram. Addressable buyers willing to pay $4+ regularly in the tens of thousands, not hundreds of thousands — sufficient for the brand at projected scale but not unlimited.
- **Consumer willingness to pay:** Empirically validated by Quest at $5.00, Jack Link's at $5.50+, Barebells at $4.00 — the Lebanese premium-protein consumer absorbs these prices.

### Section 9 — Gross Margin Verification

At original $3.00 RRP and 32% nutrition-store margin: wholesale $2.04, at 52% GM target cost = $0.98 — TIGHT and only achievable with imported beef + lean ops, IMPLAUSIBLE with local premium beef.

At recommended $4.50 RRP and 32% margin: wholesale $3.06, at cost $1.20 (base case) GM = **60.8%**; at cost $1.40 (premium local beef) GM = **54.2%** — at study target.

**MENA FMCG snack benchmark:** 45–60% GM at manufacturer level for premium-positioned brands. The 48–55% target is industry-standard **but only achievable with the corrected RRP, not the original.**

### Section 10 — GCC Export Economics (Phase 4)

- **ESMA / UAE halal:** Per ProspectX industry guide, end-to-end halal certification for full European/multi-product facility runs €23,000–€38,000 first year. For a Lebanese SME with a single product line via SGS Lebanon (already SASO/ESMA-approved), $5,000–$12,000 first year is realistic. Annual maintenance €6,000–€9,000 for full programmes.
- **Dubai Municipality FIRS:** 2–4 weeks typical, $200–$800 per SKU including DCL lab testing.
- **UAE supermarket margins:** Bagason Group benchmark "25–40%" — **VALIDATES** study assumption. Listing fees standard for new entrants.
- **GCC distributor margin:** 20–30% UAE; 25–35% KSA (longer payment terms).
- **Landed cost Beirut → Dubai:** Sea freight $50–$100/m³; air $4–$7/kg; customs 5% + 5% VAT. For 40g product, landed uplift $0.15–$0.25/unit by sea, $0.40–$0.70/unit by air.
- **Saudi SFDA:** Per PharmaKnowl SFDA fee schedule (Aug 2025): standard food products carry no SFDA registration fee, but "Special food registration (With vitamins or special formulas): SAR 5,000/product application" (~$1,333) and food supplements SAR 5,000/item — beef jerky with protein/macro health claims may classify as "special". Budget $1,500–$3,000/SKU for SFDA path.

### Section 11 — Lebanon-Specific Financial Risk

- **USD/LBP:** Per Wikipedia citing BdL Sayrafa: "the parallel market rate has stopped fluctuating to reach a rate of LL 89,500 per US dollar" — locked at ~89,500 since Dec 2023 peg reset. FX risk for USD-denominated business model is now LOW, materially better than 2020–2022. However, this stability depends on continued central-bank policy and political conditions.
- **Banking:** Continued capital controls (Circular 158, "lollar" accounts) mean local banks unreliable for USD. Most SMEs run cash USD, OMT/Whish wallets, or offshore banking. Payments to international suppliers (Turkish packaging, international halal bodies) typically require offshore wire/crypto. **Budget $1,500–$3,000 in offshore banking setup; not currently in study.**
- **Import/export:** No restrictions on beef ingredients (Lebanon imports beef from Brazil, Argentina, India, US per Carrefour/Spinneys origin labels) or packaging. Halal slaughter compliance + importer-name labelling per Common Decision 1/1 (May 2018) mandatory.
- **Power:** Per This Is Beirut (2025), "With a commercial electricity rate of $0.247 per kilowatt-hour (kWh) in 2025, Lebanon tops the list in the Arab region" — exceeding Morocco ($0.116/kWh) and UAE ($0.110/kWh). Per L'Orient Today citing the Ministry of Energy and Water for April 2025: "The kWh price is set at LL 28,591 (approximately $0.32)" for private generator supply. For a small jerky operation with energy-intensive drying ovens, the blended effective rate is ~$0.27–$0.32/kWh — materially higher than regional peers. Budget **$500–$1,200/month at Tier 2 scale**, explicitly carved out in COGS.
- **Inflation:** Lebanese food CPI has stabilized substantially since the 2024 peg but local input prices remain dollar-linked and volatile ±5–10%. Plan annual COGS uplift 5–8%.

### Section 12 — Benchmarking

- **Comparable brands:** LivGood Lebanon has scaled the premium-snack category as a curated marketplace — useful channel benchmark. Local protein/health snack brands (Fuel to Go, FitSpo, Healthbox) demonstrate demand at $3–$5 price points, validating consumer willingness to pay in the corrected band.
- **MENA jerky:** Wild West has scaled UAE-only — Lebanon is green-field for a Halal-native locally-produced jerky. Strategic ADVANTAGE for Strike Bites: no incumbent local meat-snack competitor.
- **CAC per retail account:** Realistic $50–$150 for nutrition shops/gyms, $300–$1,000 for modern trade. Make explicit in deck.
- **MoPH approval timeline:** No published timeline; industry practice 2–4 months for analogous food categories. Plan 90 days from submission.

## Recommendations

### Immediate corrections required before board meeting (Tier 2 reference):

1. **Reset retail price from $2.50–$3.50 to $4.00–$4.75**, with $4.50 as modelling midpoint. Single most consequential change. Restores GM target, aligns with verified competitors ($5.62 Jack Link's, $5.00 Quest, $4.00 Barebells), and meaningfully improves projected economics.

2. **Halve the M6 revenue projection** from $18K–$28K to **$9K–$14K**, retaining higher figure as upside case contingent on accelerated account ramp + >25% repeat rate. Move breakeven from Month 5–7 to **Month 8–11**.

3. **Flag MoPH food registration and GS1 Lebanon fees as OPEN line items**. Get binding quotes from MoPH Food Safety Department and GS1 Lebanon (customers@gs1lb.org / +961 1 744 161) before final presentation. Hold $1,500 contingency reserve.

4. **Add explicit budget lines currently MISSING from Tier 2:**
   - Modern-trade listing fee allowance: $1,500–$3,000 if Spinneys/Carrefour pursued
   - Offshore banking setup: $1,500–$3,000
   - Sampling campaign reserve: $500–$1,500
   - GCC-portable halal contingency (if Phase 4 within 12 months): +$5,000–$10,000
   - Power/generator explicit line: $500–$1,200/month at Tier 2 scale

5. **Adjust channel margin assumptions:**
   - Gyms 25–30% → **30%**
   - Distributor 15–20% → **20%**
   - Add modern-trade promotional investment 3–5% of channel revenue

### Staged trigger points for moving between tiers:

- **Stay at Tier 1 (Lean) if:** MoPH or GS1 quotes come back materially higher than $1,500 combined, OR initial brand sample testing fails to validate >$4 willingness-to-pay among 30+ target consumers.
- **Move to Tier 2 (Standard, recommended) when:** MoPH approval secured for first 2 SKUs, AND 15+ pre-launch LOIs from gym/nutrition shop accounts validated.
- **Move to Tier 3 (Full Market Entry) when:** M3 sell-through data shows >1.5 units/account/day sustained AND repeat purchase rate >20%. Tier 3 should only trigger against demonstrated traction, not as launch decision.

### Strategic positioning:

Position Strike Bites as **"Lebanon's first Halal-native premium beef jerky"** — explicitly contrasting Jack Link's (US import, MrBeast packaging signals off-brand for Lebanese gym customer) and sugary protein bars (Quest, Barebells contain glycerin, maltitol, sucralose). The "zero added sugar, real meat, made in Lebanon" framing supports both the $4.50 RRP and the path to GCC export. Trilingual packaging is a meaningful asset for GCC scaling — highlight as structural advantage.

## Caveats

- **MoPH food registration fee and GS1 Lebanon fee are NOT publicly verifiable.** The figures in the original study are placeholder estimates, not validated quotes. Both must be confirmed by direct outreach before the board meeting. Treating any specific USD figure as verified would be a finance integrity failure.
- **Carrefour Lebanon shelf prices** were derived from a search-result snippet (461,446 LBP for Jack Link's Teriyaki 40g) because the site blocks automated fetch. The LivGood Lebanon price of $5.62 (Shopify metadata) is the highest-confidence verified data point. Jack Link's Teriyaki on Carrefour Lebanon shows OUT OF STOCK, indicating distribution friction in mass channel.
- **Beef jerky velocity benchmarks** in the Lebanese specialty channel are estimated by analogy to comparable MENA protein-snack categories; no published Lebanese-specific velocity data exists. The 1.5 units/account/day base case is a conservative analyst estimate, not a measured figure.
- **CrossFit gym count** in Lebanon is estimated from the CrossFit affiliate locator; a precise registered gym count from a Lebanese Ministry of Youth and Sports source was not available.
- **Tier 1 ($18,500) is achievable only on a lean, single-flavor, founder-operated basis without sales rep, without modern-trade listings, and without sampling at scale.** Proof-of-concept budget, not market-entry budget. Tier 2 remains the realistic launch budget once corrections are applied.
- **Lebanese political and security risk** is not modelled in the financial study. Any escalation of regional conflict (non-trivial probability through 2026) would materially impair logistics, banking, and discretionary spend. Hold minimum 15% contingency on Tier 2 = ~$6,300 beyond headline $42,000.
- **Halal certification scope ambiguity** — the study does not distinguish Lebanese-domestic Dar Al-Fatwa endorsement (sufficient for Lebanon sales) from GCC-portable certification via SGS/SASO-approved bodies (required for Phase 4 UAE/KSA export). Different cost categories; split in the deck.
- **Lebanon power costs are the highest in the Arab region** at $0.247/kWh commercial (This Is Beirut, 2025) plus $0.32/kWh generator rate (L'Orient Today citing Ministry of Energy and Water, April 2025) — energy-intensive operations like meat drying must explicitly model this; do not assume regional FMCG cost benchmarks.
